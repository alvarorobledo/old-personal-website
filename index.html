More About Me

I am a final year Electrical & Electronic Engineering with Management student at Imperial College London. I am interested in machine learning and start-ups.

Hey there!

My name is Alvaro. I was born and raised in Spain, and lived in Canada for a year before landing here in London for university.

I will be graduating in June 2019 with an MEng degree in Electrical & Electronic Engineering with Management from Imperial College London.

I have undertaken internships in start-ups (Traventia), research (UROP) and investment banking (Morgan Stanley). I can speak Spanish and English fluently.

As I grew up, I developed an obsession to quantify and compare everything around me. This fascination with data ended up transforming into my two main current interests: statistical models and machine learning.

My mid-term goal: to successfully launch my own start-up.

VRCafé

Entrepreneurship

Doora - Smart Door Lock

Project

Doora is a simple, easy to install add-on for your home door. It can detect whether your door is locked/unlocked, as well as open/closed. Doora was born as a piece of coursework for the Embedded Systems module. Visit our website for more info.

UROP

Machine Learning

I looked at the optimisation of Machine Learning algorithms applied to Image Recognition using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). Brushless Motor

Project

As part of the Embedded Systems coursework, we (a group of 3) built a brushless motor capable of spinning for a specific angular velocity and/or number of rotations, which can change real-time. The motor can do all of this while executing a bitcoin mining script in the background. Check out the project Github repository here.

EEBug

Project

My first ever project at Imperial. In a group of 3, we built a line-following robot capable of crossing a circuit and performing a spiral movement afterwards. To achieve this, we used both analogue (MOSFETs) and digital (microprocessor) electronics.

GPS Pet Tracker

Project

Thousands of pets are lost or abandoned by their owners every day. To tackle this, we built an autonomous IoT GPS Pet Tracker which featured energy harvesting techniques to recover energy from the pet’s movements. Check out our project proposal and final report.
